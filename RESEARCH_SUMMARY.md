## Key Research Findings

### 1. Optimal Training Volume

**Research Base:** Pelland et al. (2024) meta-regression of 67 studies, 2,058 participants

- **Minimum Effective Volume (MEV):** 4-6 sets/week per muscle (growth threshold)
- **Optimal Range (MAV):** **12-20 sets/week per muscle** (maximum adaptive volume)
- **Maximum Recoverable Volume (MRV):** 20-30+ sets/week (highly individual)

**Muscle-Specific Recommendations:**
| Muscle Group | MEV | MAV | MRV |
|--------------|-----|-----|-----|
| Chest | 10 sets/week | 12-20 sets/week | 22 sets/week |
| Back | 10 sets/week | 14-22 sets/week | 24+ sets/week |
| Shoulders | 12 sets/week | 15-20 sets/week | 26 sets/week |
| Quads | 8 sets/week | 12-18 sets/week | 20+ sets/week |
| Hamstrings | 6 sets/week | 8-14 sets/week | 16 sets/week |
| Biceps | 8 sets/week | 14-20 sets/week | 26 sets/week |
| Triceps | 6 sets/week | 10-14 sets/week | 18 sets/week |

### 2. Training Frequency

**Research Base:** Schoenfeld et al. (2019), Nuckols (2020)

- **Training 2x/week per muscle > 1x/week** (significant advantage)
- **2x vs 3x/week:** Minimal difference when volume equated
- **Optimal:** 1.5-2x per week per muscle group

**Practical Application:**
- Full Body (3 days): Each muscle 1.5-3x/week
- Upper/Lower (4 days): Each muscle 2x/week ✓ **OPTIMAL**
- Push/Pull/Legs (6 days): Each muscle 2x/week ✓ **OPTIMAL**

---

### 3. Rep Ranges for Hypertrophy

**Research Base:** Schoenfeld et al. (2021) - "Re-examination of Repetition Continuum"

**Key Finding:** Hypertrophy occurs across 5-30 reps when sets taken near failure

**BUT - Optimal Ranges:**
- **Primary: 6-12 reps @ 60-80% 1RM** (best stimulus-to-fatigue ratio)
- **Secondary: 12-20 reps @ 50-70% 1RM** (metabolic stress, variety)
- **Occasional: 4-6 reps @ 80-85% 1RM** (Type II fiber emphasis)

**Critical Factor:** Proximity to failure matters more than exact rep range

---

### 4. Proximity to Failure (RIR/RPE)

**Research Base:** Hermann, Schoenfeld et al. (2025), Robinson et al. (2024)

**Major Finding:** Training to failure vs 2 RIR produced similar hypertrophy

**Optimal Approach:**
- **Most sets: 1-3 RIR (RPE 7-9)**
- **Occasional sets to 0 RIR** (last set of exercises)
- **Avoid failure on:** First sets, heavy compounds (injury risk)

**Benefits of NOT Training to Failure:**
- Better recovery between sessions
- Less muscle soreness
- More total weekly volume possible
- Lower injury risk
- Sustainable long-term

---

### 5. Rest Periods Between Sets

**Research Base:** Singer, Wolf, Schoenfeld et al. (2024) - Frontiers in Sports and Active Living

**Hypertrophy Research:**
- **Optimal: 1-2 minutes minimum**
- **Compound exercises: 2-3 minutes** (allows quality of next set)
- **Isolation exercises: 1-2 minutes**
- **Resting <1 minute may impair hypertrophy**

**OLD MYTH BUSTED:** "30-60 seconds for hypertrophy" is INCORRECT per 2024 research

**For Strength:**
- **3-5 minutes** for main lifts
- Full recovery > rushed workouts

---

### 6. Exercise Selection Hierarchy

**Research Base:** NSCA, ACSM, Israetel (RP), Schoenfeld, Helms

**Optimal Distribution:**
- **70-80% compound movements**
- **20-30% isolation movements**

**The Big 5 (Non-Negotiable):**
1. Squat (or variation)
2. Deadlift/Hip Hinge
3. Horizontal Press (Bench Press)
4. Overhead Press
5. Horizontal Pull (Row/Pull-up)

**Movement Patterns to Cover:**
- Squat (knee-dominant)
- Hip Hinge (hip-dominant)
- Horizontal Push & Pull
- Vertical Push & Pull
- Loaded Carry/Anti-rotation

**Isolation Role:**
- Address muscle imbalances
- Target muscles inadequately hit by compounds
- Regional muscle development
- Lower systemic fatigue

---

### 7. Exercise Order Within Workout

**Evidence-Based Sequence:**
1. **Power exercises** (Olympic lifts, plyometrics) - when fresh
2. **Heavy compound exercises** (squat, deadlift, bench)
3. **Accessory compounds** (RDLs, lunges, dumbbell presses)
4. **Isolation exercises** (curls, raises, extensions)

**Rationale:** Maximize performance when CNS is fresh, minimize injury risk

---

### 8. Progressive Overload Implementation

**Research Base:** Plotkin et al. (2022), Chaves et al. (2024)

**Key Finding:** Both load progression AND rep progression produce similar results

**Recommended: Double Progression Method**
1. Start at lower end of rep range (e.g., 6 reps)
2. Add reps each week (6 → 7 → 8 → 9 → 10 → 12)
3. When you hit top of range on ALL sets, increase weight
4. Drop back to lower reps, repeat

**Example:**
- Week 1-5: 135lbs × 6,7,8,9,10,12 reps (progressing)
- Week 6: 140lbs × 6 reps (increase weight, reset)

---

### 9. Periodization Strategies

**Research Base:** Williams et al. (2017) meta-analysis

**For Hypertrophy:**
- **Linear vs DUP vs Non-periodized:** NO significant difference in muscle growth
- **Total volume matters more than periodization structure**

**For Strength:**
- Periodization > Non-periodized
- Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP) slight edge

**Practical Recommendation:**
- Focus on progressive volume accumulation over 4-6 weeks
- Deload every 6 weeks
- Don't overcomplicate for hypertrophy

---

### 10. Deload Protocols

**Research Base:** Pritchard et al. (2024), Jukic et al. (2023)

**Competitive Athletes Use:**
- **Frequency:** Every 4-6 weeks (average 5.6 weeks)
- **Duration:** 5-7 days (average 6.4 days)

**Method A - Volume Deload (Recommended):**
- Reduce sets by 40-60% (e.g., 4 sets → 2 sets)
- Maintain intensity (same weights)
- Keep same exercises and frequency

**Method B - Full Rest Week:**
- Complete break from training
- Use when particularly fatigued or experiencing joint issues

**Deload Indicators:**
- Performance decline for 2+ sessions
- Persistent soreness >72 hours
- Joint pain or aches
- Sleep quality deterioration
- Decreased motivation

## Red Flags & Adjustments

### Increase Volume If:
- No progress for 3+ weeks
- Recovery is excellent
- Training feels too easy at 3 RIR

### Decrease Volume If:
- Chronic fatigue
- Sleep quality declining
- Strength decreasing
- Soreness lasting 4+ days
- Joint pain

### Change Exercises If:
- Pain during movement (not soreness)
- Plateau for 6+ weeks on specific exercise
- Equipment unavailable
- After 12-16 weeks (variety)
